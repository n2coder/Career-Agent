# India IT Market and Career Intelligence Pack (Snapshot: February 2026)

Last updated: February 12, 2026
Purpose: High-signal, practical context for India-focused IT career guidance.

## 1) India IT Industry Snapshot

- The Indian technology industry is estimated at about USD 283B in FY25, with exports around USD 224B and domestic tech revenue around USD 58B.
- Industry employment is about 5.8M with net hiring reported at roughly 126K in FY25.
- FY26 outlook from industry coverage points to approaching USD 300B scale, with AI-led delivery, engineering R&D, cloud, cybersecurity, and GCC expansion as key drivers.

Implication for candidates:
- Hiring is selective but active in high-skill tracks.
- Practical AI + engineering + cloud skills are now central for mid/high salary bands.

## 2) Hiring Momentum (2025-2026 Signals)

### Broad market
- Naukri JobSpeak showed IT hiring recovery phases through 2025, with stronger movement in AI/ML categories.
- June 2025 IT hiring was reported up YoY, while AI/ML demand spikes remained a repeated theme in monthly updates.

### Role-level demand acceleration
- AI/ML Engineer demand surges were repeatedly reported across 2025 monthly reads.
- Data engineering, search engineering, cloud, and cybersecurity-adjacent roles stay resilient where business impact is measurable.

### Experience-band pattern
- Hiring remains strongest for mid and senior talent in many sectors.
- Fresher opportunities exist, but conversion improves sharply with portfolio + internships + skills proof.

## 3) Skills That Convert to Interviews in India (2026)

### Core technical stack
- Python, SQL, and API/backend fundamentals.
- Cloud: AWS/Azure/GCP fundamentals + one cloud deeply.
- Data + AI: Pandas/NumPy, model lifecycle basics, prompt engineering, RAG patterns.
- DevOps foundations: Linux, Git, CI/CD, Docker, observability basics.
- Security baseline: secure coding, IAM, secrets handling, vulnerability basics.

### High-value combinations
- AI + backend (FastAPI/Django + vector search + production API design).
- Data engineering + cloud (ETL/ELT + warehousing + orchestration).
- Cybersecurity + cloud (cloud security posture + detection + hardening).
- Platform/SRE + automation (IaC + monitoring + incident response).

### Proof over buzzwords
- Recruiters increasingly value deployable projects, measurable outcomes, and GitHub quality over certificate volume alone.

## 4) Company Landscape to Target

### Tier 1 IT services
- High volume, broad entry points, strong process maturity.
- Good for freshers and career pivots when project allocation is strong.

### Product companies and digital-first firms
- Higher bar on systems and problem solving.
- Better compensation upside for strong execution and ownership.

### GCC ecosystem (major growth zone)
- GCCs continue to be a major demand engine in top cities.
- Hiring emphasis: engineering depth, domain specialization, AI and platform capabilities.

## 5) City Strategy: Jobs, Cost, and Best Corridors

Note: Cost data below is crowd-sourced market signal (Numbeo snapshots), not regulatory data.

## Bengaluru
- Demand signal: Continues to lead or co-lead office/tech leasing and GCC demand.
- Job-hunt corridors:
  - Outer Ring Road (Bellandur/Marathahalli ecosystem)
  - Whitefield and ITPB belt
  - Electronic City
  - Manyata / North Bengaluru corridor
  - Koramangala-HSR startup corridor
- Cost signal (Numbeo latest snapshot):
  - 1BR city centre approx INR 30k/month
  - 1BR outside centre approx INR 18k/month

## Hyderabad
- Demand signal: Strong GCC and tech absorption; among top hiring metros for AI/ML spikes in several 2025 snapshots.
- Job-hunt corridors:
  - HITEC City / Madhapur / Gachibowli
  - Financial District / Nanakramguda
  - Raidurg-Kondapur belt
- Cost signal (Numbeo latest snapshot):
  - 1BR city centre and outside-centre trends generally below Bengaluru and Gurgaon in many ranges.

## Pune
- Demand signal: Strong H1 2025 leasing growth; GCC activity and pre-commitments noted in market reports.
- Job-hunt corridors:
  - Hinjawadi
  - Kharadi / EON belt
  - Baner-Balewadi-Wakad stretch
  - Magarpatta / Hadapsar
- Cost signal (Numbeo latest snapshot):
  - Mid-tier metro cost profile, generally below Mumbai and Gurgaon.

## Chennai
- Demand signal: Stable large-enterprise and services demand; meaningful AI/ML hiring pockets in 2025 snapshots.
- Job-hunt corridors:
  - OMR / Sholinganallur / Perungudi
  - Guindy-Mount corridor
  - Ambattur and surrounding industrial-tech clusters
- Cost signal (Numbeo latest snapshot):
  - Competitive living costs versus Bengaluru/Mumbai/Gurgaon.

## NCR (Gurgaon + Noida + Delhi access)
- Demand signal: Strong share in office leasing and unicorn hiring updates.
- Job-hunt corridors:
  - Gurgaon Cyber City/Cyber Hub, Golf Course Road, Udyog Vihar
  - Noida Sector 62-63 and Noida Expressway
  - Greater Noida tech/industrial pockets
- Cost signal (Numbeo latest snapshot):
  - Gurgaon often among highest rent bands in NCR.
  - Noida comparatively lower for many categories.

## Mumbai
- Demand signal: Strong BFSI-tech and enterprise presence; consistent top-city relevance.
- Job-hunt corridors:
  - BKC
  - Powai
  - Lower Parel-Worli corridor
  - Navi Mumbai tech and enterprise pockets
- Cost signal (Numbeo latest snapshot):
  - Among the highest rent and total monthly burn in India tech hubs.

## 6) Salary and Compensation Reality (2025-2026)

- India overall salary increase projections are around 9 percent for 2026 in major surveys.
- Variation by sector is significant.
- Technology consulting/services can see lower average hikes than high-growth niche tracks.
- GCC, engineering design, and niche capabilities often command stronger compensation growth than generic profiles.

Practical compensation advice:
- Optimize for total comp and role quality, not CTC headline alone.
- Validate fixed vs variable share and vesting conditions.
- For switches, build leverage through 2-3 strong interview loops before negotiation close.

## 7) Career Advice by Experience Level

## Freshers (0-2 years)
- Primary objective: employability velocity.
- Build 3 job-ready projects aligned to target role.
- Focus stack depth over breadth (one backend, one cloud, one data/AI extension).
- Apply in weekly sprints with referral-first strategy.

## Early-Mid (2-5 years)
- Primary objective: specialization and higher-value ownership.
- Move from task execution to service ownership and measurable business impact.
- Add system design + production readiness + cost/performance thinking.
- Switch when role scope and pay are both improving.

## Mid-Senior (5-9 years)
- Primary objective: architecture + mentorship + domain edge.
- Build a narrative: scale problems solved, failures handled, and outcomes shipped.
- Target GCC/product tracks where depth is rewarded.

## Senior (9+ years)
- Primary objective: strategic scope and compounding influence.
- Choose IC-vs-management track consciously.
- Prioritize organizations with clear architecture ownership and talent density.

## 8) 30-60-90 Day Job Hunt Plan (India IT)

## First 30 days
- Pick one target role and one backup role.
- Rebuild resume around outcomes, not duties.
- Publish one flagship project case study.

## 31-60 days
- 80-120 targeted applications with referral-first outreach.
- Interview prep loop: DSA/system design/domain rounds per role.
- Weekly mock interviews and feedback correction.

## 61-90 days
- Parallel final-round pipelines in at least 3 companies.
- Compensation benchmarking and offer comparison.
- Decide by role trajectory + manager quality + learning curve.
