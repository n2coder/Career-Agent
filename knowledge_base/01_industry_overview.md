# Indian IT Industry Overview (Updated: February 12, 2026)

## Executive Snapshot
- India technology industry (including hardware) is estimated around USD 283B in FY25.
- Exports are estimated around USD 224B in FY25.
- Domestic technology market is estimated around USD 58B in FY25.
- Industry employment is around 5.8M with net hiring around 126K in FY25.

## What This Means for Job Seekers
- Hiring has improved versus slowdown periods, but screening is more selective.
- Employers prioritize impact skills: AI-enabled engineering, cloud delivery, cybersecurity, and platform reliability.
- Mid-level talent with production experience generally has better conversion than profile-only candidates.

## Demand Segments in India
- IT Services and Consulting: large volume, broad stack exposure, strong process maturity.
- Product and SaaS Companies: higher compensation upside, higher evaluation bar.
- GCCs (Global Capability Centers): strong and sustained demand across engineering, data, and security roles.
- Startup and Digital-first Firms: fast learning, ownership-heavy roles, variable compensation structures.

## Hiring Patterns by Experience
- 0-2 years: opportunities exist but require project proof and fundamentals.
- 2-5 years: strongest window for role upgrades and salary jumps.
- 5-9 years: architecture, ownership, and cross-functional execution become core.
- 9+ years: domain depth, org influence, and technical strategy matter most.

## High-Value Role Families
- AI/ML Engineering and Applied AI Systems
- Data Engineering and Analytics Engineering
- Cloud/DevOps/SRE and Platform Engineering
- Cybersecurity Engineering and Cloud Security
- Backend Engineering with distributed systems fundamentals

## Practical Positioning Strategy
- Build a portfolio with measurable outcomes, not just tutorials.
- Align resume to one primary role and one secondary role.
- Show deployment evidence: architecture notes, metrics, and tradeoff decisions.
